Back stretch

Note
If you are a new or expectant mother, or suffer from a back injury, please consult your doctor before performing this exercise.
close
  • Sit on the front of your chair - make sure your chair is stable on the floor and isn’t going to move whilst you exercise.
  • Place both feet flat on the floor - if you normally use a footrest you may need to adjust your chair.
  • Tuck your chin towards your chest, and as you breathe out allow the rest of your back to slump forwards, rounding your spine.
  • Hold this position for one to three breaths.
  • Lift your head and straighten your back and lift your breastbone to arch your upper back. Keep your chin low so as not to put pressure on your spine from the weight of your head.
  • Repeat exercise up to 5 times by alternating between the slump forwards and the arching back, feeling your spine move gently.
  • Finish by returning to a neutral sitting position.
