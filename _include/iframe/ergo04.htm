Using your mouse correctly

Use your forearm to move the mouse up and down and to the left and right. Keep your arm relaxed. Don't use the mouse with your arm fully extended as this causes muscle strain in the shoulder area.

close

Don't move your wrists to the left or right to use a mouse.

Instead, use your forearm to move the mouse left or right (as shown in animation 1) to ensure wrists remain straight. Wrists used at an angle cause aches and discomfort.

close

Don't leave your hand on the mouse when not in use. Holding your mouse for a long period of time requires you to maintain a static force that can cause aches and discomfort.

Instead, release your grip and roll your hand off to the side to rest.

close

Don’t grip the mouse too tightly - rest your fingers lightly on the buttons and press gently. Check that holding the mouse doesn’t cause your shoulders and arms to tense. Let go of the mouse and try some shoulder shrugging if you feel tension building up.
